### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** You have a project in Marion County where the UAL comes back with "Benefitting" for water and "Nitrogen" for sewer. Both trigger client holds. Walk us through the complete process: how you communicate the extra costs, what the actual dollar amounts typically look like, how the client hold works through Client Relations, and what happens if the client refuses both.

---

**Q22.** A SECO underground electric installation comes back with a $9,500 invoice — far above the typical under-$1,000 amount. This is because there's no power pole near the property. Walk us through how you handle this: the client hold process, whether the project can proceed without electricity, and what alternatives exist.

---

**Q23.** You're managing utilities for 25 active houses across Marion, Citrus, and Charlotte counties simultaneously. Each county has different utility companies, different processes, different portals, and different quirks. Describe how you keep track of everything. What's your system? What falls through the cracks most often?

---

**Q24.** The Alex Shore portal (a county utility portal) has been having problems since a migration and you can't access account information. You have 5 invoices that need to be paid but you can't verify the amounts online. Describe your workaround process. Who do you call? How do you verify before paying?

---

**Q25.** A septic DOH inspection comes back with a partial approval — the inspector notes a "pendency of water line" because the water meter hasn't been installed yet. But the water meter installation is estimated at 3-6 months away. The house construction can't wait that long. Walk us through how you manage this dependency — what can proceed, what's blocked, and how do you coordinate the timing?

---

**Q26.** Describe the most confusing or unusual utility situation you've encountered where the standard process didn't apply. For example: a lot with unusual geography, a county that changed its rules mid-project, a utility company that merged or changed portals, or a project that needed both a well AND a septic AND was in a flood zone. How did you figure out what to do?

---

**Q27.** You discover that a surveyor was supposed to re-mark stakes on a lot before septic installation, but it wasn't done. The septic company shows up to install and can't proceed. The supervisor says the stakes were there last week but seem to have been removed (maybe by the neighboring property's construction crew). Walk us through the scramble to get this resolved without losing the septic company's scheduled slot.

---

**Q28.** Explain the difference between how utility processes work in each county Lakeshore operates in. For each county (Marion, Citrus, Charlotte, Lake, Sumter), describe: the main utility providers, any unique quirks or requirements, typical wait times, and which county is the most difficult to work with and why.

---

**Q29.** A construction project has reached the Final Electric inspection stage, but there's a problem: the impact fee hasn't been paid yet because Financeiro had a processing delay. Without the impact fee paid, the inspection can't happen, and without the inspection, the CO can't be issued. The closing date with the buyer is in 2 weeks. Walk us through the emergency coordination to unblock this.

**Q30.** If you could redesign the entire utility tracking process from scratch (no more triple-entry, no more portal problems, no more information scattered across spreadsheets and BT and Slack), what would the ideal system look like?

### Section B: Process & Decision-Making (Open Response)

**Q11.** Walk us through your BlueBeam calibration process step by step. Which reference dimensions do you prefer to use, and what do you do if the engineering drawings don't have clear reference dimensions? How do you verify the calibration is correct?

**Q12.** Describe how you calculate concrete volume for a slab using BlueBeam. What measurements do you take, what assumptions do you make about thickness, and how do you account for footers and thickened edges? Where do errors most commonly occur?

**Q13.** When you're doing a takeoff for blocks (CMU), explain how you differentiate between regular blocks, lintel blocks, rough blocks, 3/4 blocks, and column blocks. What information on the plans tells you which type goes where?

**Q14.** Describe the process of measuring roof area for shingles in BlueBeam. How do you account for the roof angle/pitch? What's the relationship between the flat projected area and the actual shingle area needed?

**Q15.** When you use Software Globin for a takeoff, how do you cross-verify those results with your BlueBeam measurements? What kinds of discrepancies do you typically find between the two tools?

**Q16.** Walk through how you handle doors and windows in a takeoff. Beyond just counting them, what additional information do you capture (sizes, types, opening direction)? Why does opening direction matter, and where does that information come from on the plans?

**Q17.** Describe a situation where the engineering drawings were ambiguous — maybe a dimension was unclear, a detail was missing, or there was a conflict between two views. How did you resolve it? Who did you contact, and how long did it take?

**Q18.** When a custom home client changes from vinyl flooring to tile mid-construction, walk through exactly which takeoff categories need to be re-done. Is it just the flooring section, or does it cascade to other items?

**Q19.** How do you handle the takeoff for exterior finishes like stucco? What measurements do you need, how do you account for window/door openings, and what's the typical waste factor you use for stucco vs. paint?

**Q20.** Describe how you communicate a completed takeoff to the Budget team. What information do they need beyond just the quantities? How do you ensure they understand any assumptions or judgment calls you made?

### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** You're doing a takeoff on a new custom home with an unusual L-shaped floor plan, a covered lanai, a three-car garage, and a pool. The plans show areas that could be interpreted as either conditioned or unconditioned space. How do you decide what to include in each material category, and who makes the final call on ambiguous areas?

**Q22.** The Budget team tells you that your block count for a spec home is 12% higher than what CEMEX quoted when they did their own vendor takeoff. Walk through your investigation process. What are the common reasons for this discrepancy? How do you determine who is correct?

**Q23.** You've just completed a takeoff for Model 1774 and saved it to Drive. Three days later, Luana tells you the engineer revised the truss layout, which changes the roof pitch on the back section of the house. List every takeoff category that might be affected by this change and explain why.

**Q24.** A new Palmer elevation is being introduced with a different facade but the same floor plan as the existing Palmer. What parts of the existing takeoff can you reuse, and what needs to be re-measured? How do you handle this efficiently without re-doing the entire takeoff?

**Q25.** You're using ChatGPT to help calculate a complex concrete pour that involves multiple footing depths, a step-down in the foundation, and an irregular lot shape. Describe exactly how you frame the problem for the AI, what information you provide, and how you verify the AI's calculation is correct.

**Q26.** The BlueBeam paid feature for linking measurements directly to spreadsheets has never been used at Lakeshore. Based on your experience, describe what you think would be needed to set this up. What would change in your workflow if measurements auto-populated into a spreadsheet instead of being compiled manually in Word?

**Q27.** You receive a floor plan for a custom home that includes a wine cellar, a home theater with acoustic insulation requirements, and a butler's pantry with commercial-grade countertops. None of these are in your standard spec home templates. Walk through how you build the takeoff for items you've never quantified before.

**Q28.** Scheduling (Mauricio) tells you he changed the block order quantity for a house because "they always need 5% more than the takeoff says." Meanwhile, there's leftover material from the last three houses of the same model sitting at the job site. How should leftover material be accounted for in future takeoffs?

**Q29.** If you had to train a new team member to do takeoffs from scratch, what are the top 5 mistakes beginners make that experienced people have learned to avoid? Be specific about each mistake and how to prevent it.

**Q30.** Looking at the full takeoff process — from receiving the engineering PDF to delivering the final quantities to Budget — where does the most time get spent? If you could automate or improve ONE part of the process, what would it be and how would you design the solution?

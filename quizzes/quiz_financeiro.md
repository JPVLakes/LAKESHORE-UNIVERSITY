### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** It's a Friday afternoon. You have 35 invoices in queue, Leo is traveling and hasn't approved the payment batch yet, and three subcontractors are texting Fernanda directly asking when they'll be paid. One of them threatens to stop work on a house that's scheduled for framing Monday. Describe how you handle this — who do you contact, what can you authorize, and what can't you do without Leo?

**Q22.** A subcontractor (Eco One Plumbing) sends you an invoice for $4,800 for plumbing rough on a house where the PO is $3,200. The invoice includes a $1,600 line item for "additional work — broken pipe repair." There's no PO or authorization for this additional work. Walk through your complete process for handling this discrepancy.

**Q23.** You discover that the same invoice number from a supplier was processed and paid twice — once last week and once this week — on two different houses. Both bills are already in QuickBooks. Describe your step-by-step recovery process. Who do you notify? How do you correct both Builder Trend and QuickBooks?

**Q24.** The Compras team made a bulk lumber purchase from 84 Lumber that needs to be split (rateio) across 6 houses. The total invoice is $37,062 but the individual house allocations need to account for different quantities delivered to each address. Walk through how you handle the cost allocation. What information do you need from Compras, and what happens if their allocation doesn't add up to the total?

**Q25.** A custom home project is going 25% over budget at the Draw 3 milestone. The client's lender is refusing to release the next draw because the cost-to-complete ratio exceeds their threshold. Meanwhile, subs need to be paid for completed work. Describe the financial juggling act — what options does Lakeshore have?

**Q26.** You're processing permits fees for 5 houses in Marion County simultaneously. Each has a different application fee, release fee, and impact fee structure. Describe how you track which fees have been paid vs. pending for each house, and how you ensure the correct amounts reach the correct county portal.

**Q27.** Describe the full lifecycle of a change order from a financial perspective. The client approved a $4,500 kitchen upgrade mid-construction. Walk through every financial system that needs to be updated, from the PO through the budget adjustment through the draw schedule through QuickBooks.

**Q28.** End of month: you need to reconcile the bank account in QuickBooks against all payments made. You notice three payments in the bank that don't have corresponding bills in QuickBooks, and two bills in QuickBooks that show as paid but don't appear in the bank. What's your investigation process for each type of discrepancy?

**Q29.** A supervisor used the field card at Home Depot for $1,200, at Lowe's for $450, and at a gas station for $85 — all in one day. The Slack receipts show the Home Depot purchase has two house numbers on it (materials split between houses), the Lowe's receipt is for a house address that doesn't exist in your system, and the gas station charge shouldn't be on the construction card. Walk through how you handle each one.

**Q30.** If you could redesign the invoice-to-payment pipeline from scratch — from the moment an invoice hits your email to the moment the check clears the bank — what would you change? Where are the biggest bottlenecks, the most manual steps, and the highest-risk points for errors? Be specific about what technology or process changes would have the biggest impact.

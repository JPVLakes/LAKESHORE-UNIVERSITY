# Company Chaplain -- Knowledge Assessment
## Lakeshore Homes Professional Development Program

*This assessment helps us understand how we support the well-being of our team and maintain the faith-based culture that makes Lakeshore special. Your thoughtful responses will help us care for every person in our company more effectively.*

---

- A) A financial review

### Section B: Process & Decision-Making (Open Response)

**Q11.** Describe the moments during a typical construction project (from permit to closing) when employees are most likely to experience stress, frustration, or burnout. At which specific phases do you see the most emotional strain on the team, and why?

**Q12.** Walk us through how you would prepare a devotional for a Monday morning meeting during a particularly challenging week -- let's say two inspections failed, a sub walked off a job, and a tropical storm is forecast for Thursday. How do you make it relevant without being tone-deaf to the stress people are feeling?

**Q13.** Describe how you balance the spiritual role with workplace boundaries. When does encouragement cross the line into something that could make an employee uncomfortable? How do you read the room, especially with team members who may not share the Christian faith?

**Q14.** A field supervisor who has been working 60-hour weeks for the past month comes to you privately and says he is thinking about quitting. He is not asking for prayer -- he is venting about burnout and feeling unappreciated. Walk through how you handle this conversation. What do you say, what do you NOT say, and do you involve anyone else?

**Q15.** Explain how you approach the Brazilian cultural dynamics within the team. Many employees are Brazilian immigrants with families back home, potentially dealing with homesickness, visa stress, or family separation. How do you provide support that acknowledges these specific challenges without overstepping?

**Q16.** Describe how you organize a milestone celebration for a completed house. What does the event actually look like -- who attends, how long does it take, what do you prepare, and how do you make it meaningful for both the construction team and the client?

**Q17.** When a safety incident happens on site (not a serious injury, but a near-miss that scared people), how do you support the affected crew in a way that complements the formal safety response from QPS and Supervisao? What does your role add that the safety process does not cover?

**Q18.** Walk us through how you handle a request for prayer from someone dealing with a personal crisis (divorce, family illness, financial trouble) that is affecting their work. How do you provide spiritual support while also recognizing that they may need professional counseling or HR intervention?

**Q19.** Describe how you make yourself accessible to field workers who are on job sites all day and may not feel comfortable coming to an office. What are the informal channels through which people actually reach out to you?

**Q20.** How do you measure the impact of your role? Not in KPIs or dashboards, but in real terms -- how do you know you are making a difference in the team's well-being and the company culture?

### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** A worker falls from a roof and is hospitalized with a broken leg and concussion. His wife speaks only Portuguese. He has two young children. The crew who witnessed the fall is shaken and refusing to go back on the roof the next day. Describe your complete response over the next 48 hours -- every action, every conversation, every coordination with other departments.

**Q22.** During a team meeting, an employee publicly challenges the practice of starting meetings with prayer, saying it makes them uncomfortable and feels exclusionary. The room goes silent. Bernardo is in the meeting. How do you respond in the moment, and how do you follow up privately with both the employee and Bernardo?

**Q23.** A long-tenured employee (8 years with Lakeshore) is being terminated due to restructuring -- not performance. This person is well-liked and is essentially a mentor figure for newer team members. The termination will be announced on Friday. Describe your complete support plan for the terminated employee, their close colleagues, and the department as a whole.

**Q24.** It is the end of a very difficult quarter. Three houses failed final inspections, two subs were fired for quality issues, and a tropical storm caused $50,000 in damage to partially-built houses. Morale across the company is at a low point. Bernardo asks you to prepare something special for the next all-hands meeting. What do you prepare, and how do you make it genuine rather than performative?

**Q25.** Two employees from different departments are having a personal conflict that started at a company social event and has spilled over into work. Both have come to you separately, each telling a different version of what happened. One is asking for prayer, the other is asking for mediation. How do you navigate this without betraying confidences?

**Q26.** A homeowner at a house handoff celebration asks you to perform a religious blessing of their new home. Their religious tradition is different from the Christian prayers you typically offer. How do you handle this respectfully while staying authentic to your role and the company's faith-based culture?

**Q27.** A worker on a job site confides in you that he believes his supervisor is creating an unsafe work environment by rushing through safety procedures to meet deadlines. He is afraid that reporting it will get him fired. He specifically says "I am telling you as a chaplain, not as a company employee." What do you do with this information?

**Q28.** Describe how you would handle the spiritual support needs during a natural disaster that affects multiple Lakeshore job sites and employees' homes simultaneously. Some employees have lost their own homes while still being expected to build homes for clients. What is your triage process?

**Q29.** Lakeshore is opening operations in a new area where the community is predominantly of a different faith tradition (or secular). How do you adapt the chaplain role for a team and client base that may not connect with Christian prayers and devotionals, while maintaining Bernardo's vision for faith-based company culture?

**Q30.** Reflect on the times you have been most effective in this role -- the moments where you felt you truly made a difference in someone's life at Lakeshore. Without naming names, describe 2-3 specific situations, what you did, and what the outcome was. Then describe a time when you felt your role was insufficient -- a situation where you wished you could have done more. What would have helped?

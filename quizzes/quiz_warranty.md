### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** A homeowner reports a plumbing leak 18 months after occupancy. The leak has caused visible water damage to the drywall and flooring. The plumbing system itself is within the 2-year mechanical warranty, but the drywall and flooring damage are cosmetic items past the 1-year mark. Walk us through how you handle this complex claim — what's covered, what's not, and how do you explain it to the homeowner?

---

**Q22.** You discover that a specific subcontractor (let's say the AC installer) has generated warranty claims on 8 out of 12 houses they worked on in the past year. Describe the process for flagging this as a systemic issue. Who needs to be informed? What data would you compile? What happens to that sub's future work assignments?

---

**Q23.** A homeowner claims their AC system failed during a July heat wave. The AC sub inspects and says the homeowner hasn't changed the filter in 6 months (against the maintenance guidance provided at closing). The homeowner insists this is a warranty issue. How do you determine fault? What documentation supports your decision? How do you handle the conversation with the homeowner?

---

**Q24.** You receive a warranty claim for foundation cracks in a house built 8 years ago. The cracks are in the garage slab and one exterior wall. This falls within the 10-year structural warranty. Walk us through the full process — assessment, sub involvement, engineering review, and cost. This is a high-value claim. Who ultimately decides if it's covered?

---

**Q25.** Describe the most common warranty claims you see across all houses, ranked by frequency. For each, explain: (a) what typically causes it, (b) which sub handles the repair, (c) average time to resolution, and (d) whether it was preventable during construction.

---

**Q26.** A house needs 5 different warranty repairs from 5 different subs (plumbing, electrical, AC, stucco, and tile). The homeowner is frustrated about scheduling 5 separate visits. How do you coordinate multiple subs for the same house? Is there a way to batch these, and what are the practical obstacles?

---

**Q27.** You inherited warranty responsibility for a house where the original repair records are incomplete — the previous team didn't properly log everything in Builder Trend. The homeowner is now making a second claim for the same issue, but you can't find evidence the first repair was ever completed. How do you handle this without admitting fault or alienating the homeowner?

---

**Q28.** A homeowner reports that their exterior stucco is cracking extensively 10 months after moving in. Upon inspection, you suspect it may be related to foundation settling rather than stucco workmanship. This could escalate from a 1-year cosmetic claim to a 10-year structural issue. Walk us through the assessment process and how you make the determination.

---

**Q29.** During rainy season, you receive a cluster of 15 warranty claims in one week — mostly related to water intrusion around windows and doors. Your regular subs are overwhelmed. How do you triage, prioritize by severity (active leak vs. cosmetic water stain), and manage the backlog without exceeding the 14-day SLA?

---

**Q30.** The CEO has mentioned that warranty costs at one house reached $14,000 — a significant loss. If you could implement a system to prevent this from happening again, what would it look like? Consider: early warning triggers, sub accountability metrics, construction quality gates, and post-closing monitoring. Be specific about what data you would track and what thresholds would trigger action.

### Section B: Process & Decision-Making (Open Response)

**Q11.** Walk us through your complete process for onboarding a new field worker. Beyond the paperwork (W-9, insurance verification), what training, introductions, and system access do they need? Who do they need to meet, and in what order?

**Q12.** Describe how you actually handle the situation when a department leader schedules a PTO in Google Calendar for an employee who has no remaining balance. How do you find out, what conversation do you have with the leader, and what happens to the employee's request?

**Q13.** When a department leader forgets to notify you about a past-date absence (since Google Calendar does not send notifications for retroactive entries), how do you typically discover it? How common is this, and what is your process for catching these gaps?

**Q14.** Explain how you handle a workers' compensation claim from start to finish. Walk us through the first phone call when an injury happens on site, the paperwork, the insurance filing, and the follow-up. Who else gets involved, and what are the time-sensitive deadlines?

**Q15.** Describe the process for verifying subcontractor insurance and compliance before they can work on a Lakeshore job site. How do you track 663+ vendor insurance statuses? What tools do you actually use, and what happens when a sub shows up to work with expired insurance?

**Q16.** When you receive a PTO request during a blackout period (construction peak season), how do you determine whether to approve it? What factors do you consider beyond the 2-week notice rule? Give us an example of a request you approved during blackout and one you denied, and explain the difference.

**Q17.** Describe how you coordinate with the Financeiro department for payroll. What information do they need from you, by when, and what are the most common payroll-related issues that come from the HR side?

**Q18.** Walk us through how you handle an employee conflict -- not a performance issue, but a personal conflict between two team members who need to work together. What is your process, who do you involve, and how do you document it?

**Q19.** Explain the difference between how you handle PTO tracking for office employees versus field workers. Are there practical differences in how absences affect operations, and does this change how you process requests?

**Q20.** Describe your process when an employee asks about their PTO balance. How quickly can you provide an answer, what tools do you use to look it up, and what are the most common sources of confusion about balances?

### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** An employee has used all 3 sick days and requests PTO for what they say is a medical follow-up appointment. They have PTO balance available but the request is for tomorrow (no advance notice) and it is during a blackout period. Their role is a field supervisor covering 15 active houses. Walk through your decision process and the conversations you need to have.

**Q22.** A field worker is injured on a job site (twisted ankle stepping off a ladder). The supervisor calls you at 3 PM. The worker says they are fine and want to keep working. Describe everything you do over the next 24 hours, including who you notify, what documentation you complete, and what your legal obligations are under Florida workers' comp law.

**Q23.** You discover that a subcontractor has been sending workers to Lakeshore job sites who are not covered under the sub's workers' compensation insurance (the sub has insurance but only for 3 workers, and they have been sending 5). What are the legal implications, what do you do immediately, and how do you prevent this going forward?

**Q24.** An employee who has been with Lakeshore for 4 years (leadership, "Red" category) is requesting 3 weeks of continuous PTO for a family emergency. They have 5 PTO days available and 2 sick days remaining. How do you handle the gap between what they have and what they need? What options can you offer, and who needs to approve what?

**Q25.** Two department leaders are both requesting PTO for the same week. One manages permits (a single-person critical role), the other manages a field supervision team with backup coverage available. How do you handle this, and what criteria do you use to make the decision if you cannot approve both?

**Q26.** Describe the complete offboarding process when an employee leaves Lakeshore -- both voluntary (they resign) and involuntary (they are terminated). What systems need to be updated, what access needs to be revoked, who needs to be notified, and what are the legal requirements?

**Q27.** A new Florida labor law takes effect that changes PTO accrual requirements for part-time employees. How do you learn about these changes, who do you consult, how do you update your processes, and how do you communicate the changes to all department leaders?

**Q28.** You are preparing for Lakeshore's growth to 500 simultaneous homes. Currently, HR is Dani and J. Costa managing ~72 employees manually with spreadsheets. Describe what would need to change -- in tools, processes, staffing, and automation -- to support a workforce of 200+ employees plus hundreds of subs.

**Q29.** An employee comes to you in confidence saying they feel they are being treated unfairly by their department leader -- not enough to file a formal complaint, but enough to affect their morale and work quality. They specifically ask you not to talk to the leader. How do you handle this sensitive situation while still addressing the underlying issue?

**Q30.** Describe the informal HR processes that exist at Lakeshore -- the things that are not in any handbook or system but are essential to how people management actually works. This might include how leaders actually communicate absences (text message at 6 AM?), how you handle employees who are technically using PTO but are still answering work calls from the field, or how you manage the cultural expectations of a team that is 70%+ Brazilian.

### Section B: Process & Decision-Making (Open Response)

**Q11.** Walk us through exactly how you determine whether a new project needs a waiver in Citrus County. What do you look at on the survey, what measurements matter, and at what point do you decide to file for the waiver vs. trying another approach?

**Q12.** When a permit application comes back denied, describe the first 30 minutes of your response. Who do you contact first? What's your process for figuring out whether this is a quick fix or something that requires engineering involvement?

**Q13.** Explain the differences in how you track permit status in Marion County vs. Citrus County. Which county's system do you find more reliable, and what workarounds do you use when the other county's system is unclear?

**Q14.** Describe what happens when you discover a permit is about to expire and you didn't get an email notification (Marion County issue). What's your emergency procedure? Have you developed any personal system to prevent this from happening?

**Q15.** For a custom home, walk us through how you handle a project that needs both a demolition permit and a construction permit. What's the sequencing, what are the gotchas, and how does this interact with HOA approval if required?

**Q16.** When Financeiro is slow to process a permit payment and the county has a deadline, what do you do? Describe any informal escalation paths or workarounds you've used.

**Q17.** Describe the "Arrows" issue in Citrus County. What triggers it, how do you resolve it, and is there a way to prevent it from happening in the first place when preparing the initial application?

**Q18.** How do you decide the order of document requests when starting a new spec home permit? Is it always the same, or do certain situations change your priority? Give an example.

**Q19.** When the surveyor (AMB or Anel) is backed up and your survey is delayed, describe how this cascades through the rest of your permit timeline. What's the latest a survey can arrive before it starts causing real problems?

**Q20.** Explain how you handle the situation when a model change happens mid-permit process (yellow tag on the spreadsheet). What documents need to be redone? How do you communicate this to the county if the application was already submitted?

### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** You have a custom home in Citrus County on a lot that needs a height waiver, the lot has potential wetlands, and the HOA requires pre-approval. The client is pushing to break ground in 6 weeks. Walk through your realistic timeline and the order you would tackle each requirement. Where are the bottlenecks that could blow the deadline?

**Q22.** A permit for a spec home in Marion County was issued 5 months ago. The supervisor tells you they haven't scheduled any inspections because of a materials delay. You know the permit expires in 30 days. The Financeiro team is on vacation this week. What do you do, step by step?

**Q23.** You receive a denial from Citrus County stating "floor plan version mismatch" but you're certain you submitted the latest version. Describe how you would investigate this. Who would you contact at the county? What's the difference between calling the reviewer directly vs. going through the portal?

**Q24.** Describe a situation where you had to coordinate between Dandara (Utilities), the septic sub, and the county simultaneously to resolve a permit issue. What was the communication flow and what would have made it easier?

**Q25.** When handling permits for a condominium project (like the ones with 16+ POs/daily logs for the same owner), what makes this different from a single-family home permit? What are the specific pain points, and what tricks have you learned to manage the volume?

**Q26.** You're applying for a permit in a county Lakeshore hasn't worked in before (Charlotte or Sumter). What's your process for figuring out the county-specific requirements? Who do you call? What documents do you gather proactively vs. waiting to be asked?

**Q27.** A supervisor in the field calls you directly saying they need a specific approved document with the county stamp urgently, but Andreia (printing) is out sick. Walk through your backup plan. Who can print? Does the format matter? What happens if you send a digital copy instead?

**Q28.** Explain the full NOC process in detail — from preparation through client signature to recording. What makes the NOC "particularly meticulous" as described? What are the most common errors you see, and what's the consequence of getting it wrong?

**Q29.** You notice that the septic permit for a house was issued by the DOH but the construction permit hasn't been applied yet because the floor plan is still pending from the architect. Meanwhile, the septic permit has a limited validity window. How do you manage the timing risk?

**Q30.** Looking at the entire permit process, if you could change ONE thing about how information flows between your department and other departments (Financeiro, Escritorio de Projetos, Utilities, Client Relations), what would it be and why? Describe the current friction and your ideal solution.

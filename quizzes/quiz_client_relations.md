### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** A client discovers that a $22,000 window order was placed with a specification different from what they selected. The windows are already manufactured and in transit. The client is furious. Walk us through exactly how you handle this — who do you involve, what options exist, who bears the cost, and how do you preserve the client relationship?

---

**Q22.** You have a client who has approved 4 different upgrades over the course of construction (new paint palette, ceiling fan, washer/dryer hookup, extra closet shelving). At the final walkthrough, the ceiling fan was never installed and the closet shelving is wrong. The sub claims they never received the order. The card fixo in Builder Trend shows the upgrades were recorded. How do you resolve this before closing?

---

**Q23.** A client's lender has delayed the draw release by 2 weeks, creating a financial hold that stops construction. The client is blaming Lakeshore for the delay. Other houses in the production line that share the same subcontractor crew are now affected. Describe how you manage the client communication, the internal coordination, and the ripple effects on other projects.

---

**Q24.** An investor client who owns 5 houses with Lakeshore is threatening to walk away from 2 unstarted contracts because of cost overruns on the 3 completed houses. The total overrun across the 3 houses is $15,000 above the original estimates. In a cost-plus model, these are legitimate costs. How do you handle this conversation, and at what point do you involve the Coordenador or C-Level?

---

**Q25.** Describe the most common client complaints you receive, ranked by frequency. For each one, explain: (a) the root cause, (b) whether it's preventable, (c) the current workaround, and (d) what systemic fix would eliminate it.

---

**Q26.** A client's house has passed all inspections and received the CO, but the client is refusing to close because they identified 15 items on their punch list that they consider unacceptable. The items range from a scratched cabinet door to a paint touch-up to a tile that's slightly uneven. Walk us through the negotiation process — what's covered, what's cosmetic, and how do you get to closing?

---

**Q27.** You receive a call from a realtor (not the direct client) who wants detailed budget and cost information about their client's house. The realtor is pushing hard for information. What are the boundaries of what you can share? When do you need client authorization? How do you handle a pushy realtor without damaging the relationship?

---

**Q28.** A Citrus County project has been delayed for 8 months due to a waiver requirement. The client signed the contract a year ago. Market conditions have changed — material costs have risen 10%, and the client is questioning whether the original contract price still applies. Walk us through how you handle this conversation using the contract terms. What clauses apply?

---

**Q29.** During the finishing phase, the client wants to visit the house every weekend to check on progress. The field supervisor is uncomfortable with this because the client is making comments directly to subcontractors about the work quality. How do you manage client site access, set expectations about the proper communication channel, and handle the supervisor's concerns?

---

**Q30.** If you could redesign the client communication process from contract signing to key delivery, what would you change? Consider the current pain points: portal issues, information scattered across systems, slow responses from field teams, duplicate communications, and lost upgrades. Be specific about what tools, processes, or handoffs need to change.

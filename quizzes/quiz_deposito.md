### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** It's Friday afternoon. You have materials staged for 5 houses for Monday delivery. A supervisor calls saying a house that wasn't on the schedule needs foundation material first thing Monday because UP inspection was just approved. The material is in stock but allocated to a different house scheduled for Wednesday. Walk us through your decision — do you reassign, and what are the consequences?

---

**Q22.** You discover that over the past 3 months, the actual material usage for a specific model house has been consistently 15-20% higher than what the takeoff specifies. This means your reorder calculations are off. How do you handle this discovery? Who needs to know? What would you change?

---

**Q23.** A new material that Lakeshore hasn't used before starts coming in — let's say a new type of insulation. You have no existing storage protocol, no reference for reorder points, and the material has specific storage requirements (must stay dry, can't be compressed). Describe how you set up the receiving, storage, and dispatch process from scratch.

---

**Q24.** During hurricane season, you receive advance warning of a major storm approaching in 72 hours. You have materials staged outside, deliveries scheduled, and 10 active construction sites. Walk us through your emergency protocol — what gets secured first, what deliveries do you cancel or expedite, and how do you communicate with all the affected teams?

---

**Q25.** The delivery truck breaks down, and repairs will take 3 days. You have 8 scheduled deliveries this week that field crews are waiting on. Describe your backup plan. What alternatives do you have? How do you prioritize which deliveries happen first with limited options?

---

**Q26.** Clauser, you've been managing this warehouse for a while. If you could redesign the entire warehouse layout and process from scratch, what would you change? What are the biggest inefficiencies in the current physical layout, and how does that impact your ability to stage and dispatch materials efficiently?

---

**Q27.** Describe a time when materials were stolen from a job site after delivery. What was the material, how was it discovered, and what process changes were implemented? What are the highest-theft-risk materials, and what precautions do you take for each?

---

**Q28.** You receive a large delivery of materials for 10 different houses all at once from a single supplier. The delivery receipt has one total quantity, but you need to split it across 10 different Zorro Sales Orders. Walk us through exactly how you handle the receiving, counting, allocation, and documentation — including what happens when the total received doesn't perfectly divide by the takeoff quantities.

**Q29.** How do you handle seasonal patterns in material demand? Which materials have the most seasonal variation? How does the type of construction phase across all active houses affect what you need to keep in stock? Give specific examples of times when seasonal demand caught you off-guard.

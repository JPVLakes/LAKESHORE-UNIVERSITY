### Section B: Process & Decision-Making (Open Response)

**Q11.** Walk us through your actual process for reviewing daily logs each morning. How do you prioritize which logs to review first? What are the first things you look for, and what patterns have you learned to spot that indicate a problem before it becomes serious?

**Q12.** Describe how you actually determine whether a daily log entry is "good enough" versus one that needs to be sent back. Give us specific examples of entries you have rejected and why, including the exact language or detail that was missing.

**Q13.** When you review a subcontractor's rework rate and it is borderline (say 12-13% over the last 30 days), how do you decide whether to flag them versus giving them more time? What context do you consider beyond just the numbers?

**Q14.** Explain how Don and Fortine plan their daily routes. What data drives the route? How do they decide which houses to skip when they cannot hit all 60? What makes them prioritize one house over another?

**Q15.** Describe the real difference between what happens at QC1 versus QC2. What specific items do you check at each, and what is the most common type of issue found at QC1 that should have been caught earlier?

**Q16.** When a supervisor marks "Checklist Done" but you know (from experience or pattern recognition) that this particular service by this particular sub often has hidden issues, what do you do? How do you balance trusting the supervisor's judgment versus your own quality instincts?

**Q17.** Walk us through the FCC (Face for Checklist Done) process from Donaldo's perspective. What does the dashboard actually look like, what does he check in the field, and what are the most common issues he finds that delay a house from getting FCC status?

**Q18.** Describe how you handle a situation where the daily log date does not match the actual work date by more than one day. How common is this, what causes it, and what is the real impact on data integrity?

### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** You notice that the Rough Plumbing inspection has failed on 3 houses in the last 2 weeks, all in Citrus County, but each house had a different plumbing sub. The failure notes all mention "pressure test" issues. What do you investigate, who do you talk to, and what is the most likely root cause that is NOT the subs' fault?

**Q22.** A house is at the Building Final inspection stage. Everything looks good except the blower door test result is marginal -- it passes but just barely meets the threshold. The inspector passes it, but you know from experience that this house will likely have comfort complaints from the homeowner. What do you do? Do you let it go since it technically passed, or do you intervene? Explain your reasoning.

**Q23.** During QC1, you find 15 items that need correction. However, 3 of those items are things that the finishing sub says are "within tolerance" and refuses to fix without additional payment. The items are: a 1/8" gap in crown molding, a slight color variation in grout, and a small chip on a cabinet door hinge. Walk through how you resolve each of these, who has authority, and where you draw the line on "good enough."

**Q24.** You are reviewing the monthly sub performance data and notice that one sub has an excellent inspection pass rate (95%) but their rework rate is also high (18%). How do you explain this contradiction, and what does it tell you about how they are actually working?

**Q25.** A supervisor has been consistently marking "Checklist Done" on services that QPS field checks reveal are incomplete about 20% of the time. This supervisor manages 30+ houses. You need to address this without creating an adversarial relationship. Describe your approach, the conversation you would have, and what systemic changes you would recommend.

**Q26.** It is hurricane season and 8 houses are in the framing/roofing phase. The weather forecast shows a tropical storm approaching in 4 days. From a quality perspective, what specific items do you need to verify on each of those houses before the storm hits? What temporary protections should be in place, and what will you need to re-inspect after the storm passes?

**Q27.** You are setting up the quality process for a new county (Sumter County) that Lakeshore is expanding into. What county-specific quality considerations do you need to research, what inspection differences might exist from Marion/Citrus, and how do you modify your checklists and monitoring for a county you have never worked in before?

**Q28.** Describe the most common "gaming" behavior you have seen or heard about in the construction quality process -- situations where subs, supervisors, or other team members find workarounds to pass inspections or get "Checklist Done" without fully completing the work. How do you detect and prevent this?

**Q29.** A homeowner contacts Lakeshore 6 months after closing with water intrusion in the master bathroom. The warranty team asks QPS to investigate. Walk through your forensic process: what records do you pull, what field checks do you perform, and how do you determine whether this is a warranty issue, a construction defect, or a homeowner-caused problem? What specifically do you look for in the original daily logs and inspection records?

**Q30.** If you could add one metric to the QPS dashboard that does not currently exist -- something you currently track mentally or on paper but cannot see in the system -- what would it be, and why would it improve quality outcomes? Describe exactly what data it would use and how you would act on it.

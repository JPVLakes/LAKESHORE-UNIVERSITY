### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** You discover that a house marked as complete in the scheduling pipeline still shows unreturned material allocations in Zorro — materials were delivered but never properly exited. The financial closing is in 3 days. Walk us through exactly how you would resolve this, who you would involve, and what the worst-case scenario is if it can't be reconciled in time.

---

**Q22.** A new lumber supplier is being considered because 84 Lumber raised prices. However, nobody at Lakeshore currently knows how to do lumber takeoffs — 84 Lumber used to handle that. How would you manage this transition? What are the risks, and what would you need in place before switching?

---

**Q23.** You're managing purchases for 30 active houses simultaneously. Three different houses need the same specialty material (a specific tile pattern) but the supplier only has enough stock for two houses, and the next batch is 6 weeks out. How do you prioritize, who do you consult, and how do you handle the scheduling impact?

---

**Q24.** Describe a situation where you had to deal with a supplier who consistently delivers late or delivers wrong specifications. What steps did you take to manage the immediate project impact, and what process do you follow to evaluate whether to keep or replace that supplier? Include how this affects cost-per-house calculations.

---

**Q25.** During peak season (March-June), material demand spikes across all active projects. Describe your strategy for ensuring material availability. How do you adjust your monthly purchasing, what safety stock levels do you target, and how do you handle competing priorities when multiple project managers need the same materials?

---

**Q26.** You receive a takeoff for a new house model that Lakeshore has never built before. The specification sheet has materials you haven't sourced before. Walk us through how you would identify new suppliers, get competitive quotes, and set up the purchasing pipeline for unfamiliar materials. What are the biggest risks?

---

**Q27.** A subcontractor on site calls and says they need a material urgently that isn't in stock and wasn't on the original takeoff. The construction phase cannot continue without it. Walk us through your decision process: when do you authorize an emergency purchase, what approvals are needed, and how do you handle the budget impact?

---

**Q28.** You notice that the same material (e.g., door knobs) is being tracked in 4 different spreadsheets with slightly different quantities. The Builder Trend PO shows one number, your Purchase spreadsheet shows another, Zorro shows a third, and the distribution spreadsheet shows a fourth. How do you determine which is correct, and what process improvements would you suggest?

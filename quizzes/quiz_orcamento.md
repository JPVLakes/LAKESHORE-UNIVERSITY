### Section B: Process & Decision-Making (Open Response)

**Q11.** Walk us through how you actually create an R3 estimate for a new custom home. What data from past projects do you look at? How do you determine the cost-per-square-foot multiplier, and how do you adjust it for different tiers (entry, mid, high-end)?

**Q12.** When a vendor quote comes in significantly higher than your budget template price, describe your decision process. At what point do you update the template vs. negotiate with the vendor vs. find an alternative supplier? Give a real example if possible.

**Q13.** Explain the CAS Creed vs. CMEX nomenclature problem. How do you match items between the two systems? Have you developed any shortcuts or reference sheets to speed this up?

**Q14.** Describe a situation where the takeoff quantities changed after you'd already finalized the budget. How did you handle it? Who did you need to notify, and what was the ripple effect?

**Q15.** How do budget templates vary between Marion, Citrus, and Charlotte counties for the same model? Beyond permit fees, what are the specific cost items that change? (Think labor rates, wind speed requirements, material costs, utility connections.)

**Q16.** When you receive a takeoff from Gabriela's team and something doesn't look right — maybe the concrete volume seems too high or the block count seems low — what's your verification process? How do you decide whether to push back or accept it?

**Q17.** Describe how you handle the MEP budget when you don't yet know whether it will be "Full MAP" or not. How does this decision from Permits affect your numbers, and when in the process do you typically get this information?

**Q18.** Walk through the process of creating POs in Builder Trend from an approved budget. What's the order you follow? Why does the order of cost codes matter, and what problems arise when POs are entered out of order?

**Q19.** How do you handle allowances for custom homes? Describe how you decide what goes into "allowance" vs. fixed pricing. What happens when the client's actual selections exceed the allowance — who makes the call on how to handle the overage?

**Q20.** When you're comparing budgets across multiple houses of the same model but in different counties, what are the telltale signs that something is wrong in one of the budgets? What do you look at first?

### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** A custom home client has changed their floor finish from vinyl to tile after the contract was signed. The takeoff team says the tile area is 1,545 sqft (previously engineered wood). Walk through every budget line item that needs to change, not just the obvious flooring cost — think labor, materials, subcontractor, schedule impact, and any other ripple effects.

**Q22.** You're budgeting a new custom home and the client wants a pool. This is not a standard Lakeshore item. Describe your complete process: who do you call for quotes, how do you structure the cost code, what allowances do you set, and how do you protect Lakeshore from cost overruns on something you don't typically build?

**Q23.** CEMEX notifies you of a 15% price increase on blocks effective next month. You have 12 active spec home budgets using CEMEX block prices. Walk through your response plan. Which budgets do you update? How do you handle houses already under contract? Who needs to know?

**Q24.** You receive three quotes for the same service: one is 30% below the other two. What's your process? How do you determine if the low quote is a great deal vs. a scope mismatch vs. a vendor that will hit you with change orders later?

**Q25.** Describe how you'd reconstruct a budget if the original file was lost and you only had the Builder Trend POs and the actual invoices. What would you be able to recover, what would be missing, and how would you estimate the gaps?

**Q26.** A supervisor purchased $2,400 in materials from Home Depot using the field card without a PO or prior approval. The receipt says "misc construction materials" with no house number. How does this get classified in the budget? What's the real process you follow vs. what the official process says?

**Q27.** You're working on the budget for a house in Charlotte County — a county Lakeshore is newly expanding into. You have templates for Marion and Citrus. Walk through how you'd build the Charlotte County template from scratch. What data do you need, where do you get it, and what are the biggest unknowns?

**Q28.** The Financeiro team tells you that actual costs on a closed house came in 18% over the R3 estimate. Walk through your analysis process. How do you determine which cost codes drove the overrun? What patterns would you look for, and what changes would you make to future R3 estimates?

**Q29.** During peak season, you need to create budgets for 8 new spec homes simultaneously, all the same model but across Marion and Citrus. Describe your strategy for efficiency. What do you template, what do you customize, and what's the minimum turnaround time if everything goes smoothly?

**Q30.** If you could redesign the entire quote-to-budget pipeline from scratch — from receiving the takeoff through finalizing the budget in Builder Trend — what would you change? Where is the most time wasted today, and what would save the most hours per week?

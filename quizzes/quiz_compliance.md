*This assessment helps us understand expertise across our team and identify areas for professional growth. Your detailed responses will help us strengthen our regulatory compliance and protect every project we build.*

### Section B: Process & Decision-Making (Open Response)

**Q11.** Walk us through your actual process for monitoring permit expiration dates across all active projects. How do you track which projects are approaching the 180-day limit, what triggers your alerts, and what is your process when a permit is within 30 days of expiring?

**Q12.** Describe the real differences in working with inspectors across Marion, Citrus, and Charlotte counties. Do different counties have different inspection cultures (strict versus lenient)? Are there specific inspectors who are known for particular interpretation of code, and how does that affect how you prepare for their visits?

**Q13.** When an inspection fails, describe the complete chain of events from the compliance perspective. Beyond the obvious re-inspection, what documentation do you create, who do you notify, and how do you ensure the failure does not create a cascading compliance issue (such as triggering the 180-day permit expiration)?

**Q14.** Explain how you handle a situation where the Florida Building Code gives a general requirement, but the county has a local amendment that either strengthens or conflicts with it. How do you determine which rule applies, and who do you consult when the interpretation is unclear?

**Q15.** Walk us through your process for verifying that Builder's Risk insurance is active and current on every project. How do you track renewal dates, what happens when a policy is about to lapse during active construction, and who is responsible for ensuring renewal?

**Q16.** Describe how you verify that all products and materials used on a job site have valid Florida Product Approval numbers. Is this checked at the ordering stage, the delivery stage, or the installation stage? What happens when a sub uses a product that is not Florida-approved?

**Q17.** Explain the practical impact of the FBC energy code requirements on construction operations. Walk us through the blower door test and duct leakage test process -- who performs them, when in the construction timeline, what are the pass/fail thresholds, and what happens when a house barely passes versus clearly fails?

**Q18.** Describe how you handle the NOC (Notice of Commencement) process. Who prepares the document, who files it, how do you verify it was recorded, and what are the legal consequences if construction begins without it?

**Q19.** Walk us through the compliance differences between building on a lot with municipal sewer versus septic. What additional inspections, permits, and documentation are required for septic systems, and which county department (versus state DOH) handles which part?

**Q20.** Describe your relationship with county building officials. How do you handle disagreements about code interpretation? Have you ever had to appeal an inspection result, and what was that process like?

### Section C: Advanced Scenarios & Edge Cases (Open Response)

**Q21.** A house in Charlotte County (150 mph wind zone) passes framing inspection, but during your review of the daily logs, you notice that the hurricane strap manufacturer is one you have never seen used before. The straps have a Florida Product Approval number, but the installation pattern does not match the county's local amendment for enhanced wind zone requirements. The sub says "the inspector approved it." What do you do?

**Q22.** A permit was issued 165 days ago and the only recorded inspection activity was a failed UP inspection at day 30. No re-inspection has been scheduled. The scheduling team says the house has been on hold due to a client financial issue. You have 15 days before the permit expires. Describe every step you take and every department you contact.

**Q23.** During a routine compliance review, you discover that 3 houses in the same community all have the same engineering firm's stamp on their foundation plans, but the soil conditions are visibly different (one lot is sandy, one is clay-heavy, one has visible rock). The plans appear to be copy-pasted rather than site-specific. What are the compliance implications, what code sections apply, and how do you investigate without creating panic?

**Q24.** A new sub starts work on a Lakeshore site. They provide a valid Workers' Compensation certificate, but you notice the certificate lists a different company name than the one on the signed contract. The sub explains it is a subsidiary or DBA. How do you verify this is legitimate, what documents do you require, and what is the worst-case scenario if you let it slide?

**Q25.** An inspector in Marion County fails a Rough Electric inspection citing "AFCI protection not provided for bedroom circuits." The electrician argues that the 2023 FBC only requires AFCI for bedrooms, not the entire house, and that the inspection is based on an outdated code. Who is right, how do you verify, and how do you handle the disagreement between your sub and the county inspector?

**Q26.** During heavy rain season, you notice that 4 houses in various stages of construction have standing water in the foundation area. No erosion control violations have been cited yet, but you know from experience that standing water can compromise the compact test results and cause foundation issues down the line. What proactive compliance steps do you take before anyone asks?

**Q27.** A homeowner files a complaint with the county 3 months after closing, claiming that their house's windows do not meet the wind zone requirements for Charlotte County. The county sends a letter to Lakeshore requesting documentation. Walk through your complete response process: what records you pull, how you verify the original compliance, and how you respond to the county.

**Q28.** Describe the gray areas in Florida building code that you encounter most frequently -- the sections where reasonable people disagree about interpretation. For each gray area, explain the conservative interpretation versus the liberal interpretation, and which one Lakeshore follows and why.

**Q29.** Lakeshore is expanding into a new county (Sumter County). Before the first permit application is submitted, what county-specific compliance research do you need to do? Walk us through everything from wind speed zones to local amendments to impact fee schedules to inspector expectations. How long does this research take, and who helps you with it?

**Q30.** If you could change one thing about how Lakeshore handles compliance -- one process, one system, one habit that would eliminate the most risk -- what would it be? Describe the current problem, your proposed solution, and quantify the risk reduction (in terms of potential fines, delays, or legal exposure avoided).

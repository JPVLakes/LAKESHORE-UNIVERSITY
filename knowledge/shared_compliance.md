## Florida Building Compliance Summary

All construction follows FBC 8th Edition (2023). Counties: Marion, Citrus, Lake, Charlotte, Sumter. 7+ inspections before CO: foundation, framing, electrical, plumbing, mechanical, insulation, final. Permits expire 180 days without inspection. NOC required before start. Verify county zoning, impact fees, wind speed per lot. Charlotte: 140-150 mph. Lien law Ch.713: NTO within 45 days.

## Active Counties

### Marion County (Ocala area -- Primary Market)
- Permit portal: Accela (https://aca-prod.accela.com/MARION/)
- Wind speed: 120 mph (ASCE 7-22, Risk Category II)
- Typical permit timeline: 4-8 weeks
- Utilities: Marion County Utilities (MCU) for served areas, or private well/septic
- Electric: Duke Energy or Ocala Electric Utility (OEU)
- Special: tree removal permit for oaks > 12" DBH, gopher tortoise survey before clearing

### Citrus County (Inverness area)
- Permit portal: Accela (https://aca3.accela.com/CitrusCounty/)
- Wind speed: 120 mph
- Typical permit timeline: 4-6 weeks
- Utilities: Citrus County Utilities or well/septic
- Electric: Duke Energy or WREC
- Special: coastal hazard zones along Gulf coast, FDOH for septic permits

### Lake County (Tavares area)
- Permit portal: own portal (https://aca.lakecountyfl.gov/)
- Wind speed: 120-130 mph (varies by zone)
- Typical permit timeline: 3-6 weeks
- Utilities: Lake County Water Authority, City of Tavares, or well/septic
- Electric: Duke Energy or SECO
- Special: lake setback requirements, karst/sinkhole geotechnical reports in certain zones

## Planned Counties

## Common Compliance Items
- **FL Building Code**: 8th Edition (2023) -- all counties
- **FPL interconnection**: required for solar installations, apply through utility provider
- **Septic vs sewer**: determined per lot -- check county utility service area maps
- **Well water vs city water**: per subdivision -- verify with county utilities department
- **Wind speed design**: affects truss engineering, window ratings (impact vs non-impact), fastener specs
- **Flood zone**: FEMA FIRM maps determine base flood elevation + freeboard requirements
- **Inspections sequence**: foundation > slab > framing > rough MEP > insulation > drywall > final
- **NOC (Notice of Commencement)**: must be recorded before any work begins
- **Permit expiration**: 180 days without inspection activity = permit expires
- **Lien law**: Florida Ch.713 -- NTO (Notice to Owner) within 45 days of first furnishing

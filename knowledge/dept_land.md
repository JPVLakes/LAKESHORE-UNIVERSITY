### Tipos de Inspecao em Site Development (Land)

**1. Pre-Development**
- Land Survey: confirmacao de limites, topografia e caracteristicas da propriedade
- Environmental Assessment: identificacao de areas sensiveis (wetlands, habitat de especies, floodplains)
- Wetlands Delineation: delimitacao detalhada de wetlands; pode exigir determinacao do USACE

**2. Grading e Preparacao de Site**
- Land Clearing Inspection: verificacao de remocao de arvores e limpeza conforme regulamentos locais
- Grading Plan Inspection: revisao do plano de grading (stormwater runoff, compactacao, inclinacao)
- Erosion Control and Sediment Management: inspecao de silt fences, straw bales, sediment basins

**3. Stormwater Management e Drenagem**
- Stormwater Management Plan: verificacao de detention ponds, swales e retention areas
- Underground Drainage System: inspecao de pipes, catch basins e dimensionamento correto de inlets/outlets

**4. Infraestrutura**
- Utility Installation: agua, esgoto, eletrica e gas com tamanho, posicionamento e teste de integridade
- Water and Sewer Systems: linhas instaladas conforme planos aprovados, testes de pressao e fluxo
- Road and Street Development: roadbed, subgrade, base material, curbs, sidewalks e stormwater
- Lighting and Electrical Infrastructure: instalacao de iluminacao de rua e eletrica subterranea

**5. Compactacao e Estabilidade do Solo**
- Soil Compaction Tests: testes em multiplas profundidades para suporte estrutural futuro
- Geotechnical Inspection: analise de solo ou agua subterranea quando necessario (estabilizacao ou drenagem)

**6. Wetlands e Conformidade Ambiental**
- Wetlands Mitigation: verificacao de plano de mitigacao (plantios e manejo de agua)
- Wildlife and Habitat Protection: medidas protetoras para especies ameacadas (ex: Florida Scrub Jays, gopher tortoises)

**7. Road e Right-of-Way**
- Right-of-Way Inspections: verificacao de roads e corredores de utilities dentro dos easements designados
- Road Base and Subgrade: materiais e compactacao corretos antes da pavimentacao final

**8. Inspecoes Finais (antes da construcao)**
- Final Grading and Drainage: todos os sistemas de drenagem funcionais e prontos para stormwater
- Final Utility Inspections: agua, esgoto, eletrica e demais utilities conectadas e funcionando
- Compliance with Zoning Requirements: setbacks, uso do solo, densidade, estacionamento, acesso e areas verdes

**9. Post-Development**
- Stormwater System Final: re-inspecao apos construcao para verificar capacidade operacional
- Landscaping Inspection: verificacao de arvores, arbustos e cobertura do solo instalados conforme aprovacao
- Final Site Inspection and Compliance: confirmacao de toda a infraestrutura, estradas, utilities e protecao ambiental

- Atualizar lista de fornecedores continuamente
- Criar Score de fornecedores: Agilidade, Qualidade, Preco

**Problema conhecido:** Alguns subs antigos passam preco por WhatsApp mas demoram para oficializar em proposta. Solucao: manter um formato de proposta salvo para o sub preencher apenas quantitativo, preco, nome da obra e data.

## Land Acquisition Process

### From Lot Identification Through Closing
```
1. Lead Capture (ClickUp)
   - Client with purchase option OR client with existing land
   - Record: client info, lot location, size, county, zoning district

2. Initial Assessment
   - Evaluate what can be built on the lot
   - Check county zoning: R-1, PUD, A-1, AG (each county has different districts)
   - Preliminary financial viability analysis

3. Due Diligence Checklist
   [ ] Zoning verification -- confirm lot zoning allows intended use
   [ ] Environmental assessment -- wetlands, protected species, floodplain
   [ ] Gopher tortoise survey -- required before clearing (FWC permit if burrows found)
   [ ] FEMA flood zone check -- FIRM maps per lot; SFHA lots need elevation certificate
   [ ] Sinkhole risk assessment -- Lake County lots may need geotechnical in karst zones
   [ ] Tree protection review -- Marion County: permit for oaks > 12" DBH
   [ ] Utility availability -- water, sewer, electric, gas access to lot
   [ ] Road/access verification -- legal access to public road
   [ ] Title search and easement review
   [ ] Soil study and topography survey

4. Engineering & Design
   - Contract soil study and topographic survey
   - Contract civil engineer for infrastructure design (underground: water, sewer, drainage, roads)
   - Review and approve engineering plans

5. Permit Acquisition
   - Submit documentation to county
   - Track approval process (timeline varies by county)
   - Receive land development permit

6. Closing
   - All due diligence items cleared
   - Engineering plans approved
   - Financial terms finalized
   - Transfer of ownership
```

## Building Transition

### Criteria for Transitioning from Land Development to Building Phase
All three conditions must be met before a lot transitions to the building team:

**1. Utilities Connected**
- Water: connected to lot boundary, pressure tested, utility letter of service
- Sewer: connected to main (or septic system approved and installed)
- Electric: service pedestal or transformer at lot boundary confirmed by utility
- Gas (if applicable): service available at lot boundary

**2. Pad Ready**
- Soil compaction tests passed at all required depths
- Final grading complete per engineering plans
- Drainage functioning as designed
- Geotechnical approval (if required by county or soil conditions)

**3. Permits Approved**
- Land development permit: approved and active
- Building permit: approved by county (coordinated with Permits dept)
- Environmental permits: all conditions met (FWC, USACE, SWPPP)

### Transition Notification
When all criteria met, Vinicius formally notifies:
- **Scheduling agent**: "Lot [ID] ready for foundation phase" with permit numbers and utility dates
- **Building/Construction team**: Infrastructure completion summary
- **Builder Trend**: Update lot status from "Land Development" to "Building"
- Include all permit numbers, utility connection confirmation dates, and compaction test results

## Land Acquisition Detail (Batch 3)

### Lot Evaluation Criteria (from Vinicius Sobrinho interview, 08/09/2025)

When evaluating a potential lot for development, Vinicius assesses the following criteria:

**Zoning Assessment:**
- Confirm lot zoning allows intended residential use (R-1, PUD, A-1, AG vary by county)
- Check for overlay districts or special zoning requirements
- Verify setback requirements for the intended house model
- Determine if HOA approval is required (condominium developments)

**Access and Infrastructure:**
- Legal access to public road confirmed via title search
- Water availability: municipal water vs well (affects cost and permit timeline)
- Sewer availability: municipal sewer vs septic system (septic more common in Marion County, adds DOH inspection requirement)
- Electric service: confirm transformer or pedestal at lot boundary
- Gas availability (if applicable)
- Road condition and whether road improvements are needed

**Environmental and Geological:**
- FEMA flood zone check using FIRM maps -- SFHA lots need elevation certificate
- Wetlands assessment: if wetlands present, may require USACE determination and mitigation plan
- Gopher tortoise survey: required in Florida before land clearing (FWC permit if burrows found)
- Sinkhole risk: Lake County lots in karst zones may need geotechnical investigation
- Tree protection: Marion County requires permit for oaks over 12" DBH

**Financial Viability:**
- Lot purchase price vs expected development cost vs projected home sale price
- Impact fee assessment: credit possible if demolishing existing structure
- Estimated infrastructure cost (underground utilities, roads, drainage)
- Timeline estimate from acquisition to building-ready

### Due Diligence Process Timeline and Checkpoints

Based on Batch 3 interview data, the typical due diligence timeline:

| Phase | Duration | Key Checkpoint |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| Initial lot assessment | 1-2 weeks | Zoning and access confirmed |
| Environmental studies | 2-4 weeks | Wetlands/gopher tortoise/flood zone cleared |
| Soil study and topography | 2-3 weeks | Geotechnical report received |
| Engineering design | 4-8 weeks | Civil engineer infrastructure plans complete |
| County permit submission | 2-6 weeks | Land development permit approved (varies by county) |
| Site work execution | 8-16 weeks | Underground infrastructure complete |
| Final inspections | 1-2 weeks | All utility connections verified, compaction tests passed |

**Total estimated timeline:** 4-9 months from acquisition to building-ready (highly variable based on county, environmental conditions, and scope)

### Transition Triggers from Land to Construction Phase

The formal transition from Land Development to Building (vertical construction) occurs when ALL of the following are complete:

1. **Utilities connected and tested:** Water pressure tested, sewer connected (or septic approved), electric service pedestal confirmed
2. **Pad ready:** Soil compaction tests passed at all required depths, final grading complete, drainage functioning
3. **Permits approved:** Land development permit active, building permit approved by county, all environmental permits conditions met
4. **Documentation package assembled:** Boundary survey recorded, environmental clearances, utility availability letters, compaction test results

**Handoff notification:** Vinicius notifies Scheduling ("Lot [ID] ready for foundation phase"), Building team (infrastructure summary), and updates Builder Trend status from "Land Development" to "Building."

9. PAD -- Grade and prepare building pad
10. COMPACT TEST -- Soil compaction certification (can fail and delay everything)
11. HUB AND TACK -- Surveyor marks house entries/exits
12. WOOD DUMPSTER -- Install plywood enclosure for debris

### Property Area Development Sub-System
The CPM defines a state machine for land development:
- **States**: Stake House Scheduling -> Loads Scheduling -> Loads Requested -> Loads In Progress -> Loads Checklist Done -> Pad Scheduled -> Compact Test
- **Key variables**: amount_requested (loads quantity by Supervisor), amount_delivered (actual delivery count)
- **Decision points**: amount_delivered >= amount_requested? Budget Evaluation? Cost Approved? Client Relations hold?
- **Cost Disapproved path**: Can lead to Project Discontinuation or Permit Canceled

## Key Glossary Terms

- **Site Work** / **Obra de Infraestrutura**: Underground infrastructure construction (water, sewer, drainage, roads)
- **Pad** / **Plataforma**: Compacted earth platform for house foundation
- **Compact Test** / **Teste de Compactacao**: Soil compaction certification -- failure delays entire schedule
- **Loads** / **Cargas de Terra**: Earth fill delivered to build up lot elevation
- **UAL (Utility Availability Letter)** / **Carta de Disponibilidade de Utilidades**: Letter confirming utility service availability
- **Gopher Tortoise** / **Tartaruga Gopher**: Protected species requiring FWC survey before land clearing
- **SWPPP** / **Plano de Prevencao de Poluicao de Aguas Pluviais**: Stormwater plan required for sites > 1 acre
- **Karst Zone** / **Zona Carstica**: Area with sinkhole risk -- may require geotechnical investigation (Lake County)
- **Setback** / **Recuo**: Minimum distance between structure and property boundary per zoning code

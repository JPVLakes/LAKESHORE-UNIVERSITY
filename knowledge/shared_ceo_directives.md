## Company Philosophy (CEO Bernardo)
- Lakeshore = scalable production system for residential real estate
- Cost-plus model: transparent execution, no risk pricing
- Client = investor, house = vehicle for capital-to-profit conversion
- Scale target: 500 homes simultaneously
- Every decision driven by ROI, cycle time, capital efficiency
- Pipeline: Opportunity -> Design -> Budget -> Contract -> Permits -> Build -> Deliver
- Concessionaire model: 51% Lakeshore / 49% operator for network expansion

## Cost-Plus Model
- Lakeshore uses cost-plus pricing, NOT fixed-price contracts
- Client pays real execution cost + Lakeshore margin (transparent)
- Budget is an ESTIMATE, not a cap -- variations are the client's exposure
- Key distinction: R3 (budget estimate at contract signing) vs Real (actual cost at completion)
- Client benefits: no inflated risk margins, pays only for what is actually built
- Lakeshore does not price risk or guess the future -- executes reality
- Change orders and material price fluctuations pass through to client with documentation
- This model requires rigorous cost tracking (see Financeiro dept) and transparent reporting

## ROI Framework
- Every operational decision is evaluated by Return on Investment
- Technology investments must show measurable return before approval
- BuilderTrend (BT) is the primary management platform -- ALL departments must use it consistently
- Data-driven decisions: if it cannot be measured, it cannot be improved
- Key metrics: cycle time per phase, cost per square foot, days from permit to CO
- Capital efficiency: minimize idle time between construction phases
- Investment priority: tools that reduce cycle time > tools that reduce headcount

## Concessionaire Model
- Expansion strategy: sub-divisions operated as concessions (dealerships)
- Structure: 51% Lakeshore / 49% local operator (concessionaire)
- Each concessionaire manages their lots within a subdivision
- Lakeshore provides: brand, systems, processes, purchasing power, oversight
- Concessionaire provides: local market knowledge, labor relationships, daily management
- Revenue split aligned with ownership percentage
- Quality control: Lakeshore QPS department audits all concessionaires
- Goal: replicate the Lakeshore production system in multiple markets without proportional headcount growth

## Scaling Strategy
- Current target: 200+ homes/year in active construction
- Long-term vision: 500-1000 homes simultaneously across multiple markets
- Standardize ALL processes across departments -- reduce variation
- Reduce dependency on individual knowledge -- document everything (Lista Mestra IT-01 to IT-11)
- Cross-train employees: no single point of failure per role
- PM + Supervisor + Monitor model allows scaling field operations
- Heavy backend team (Brazil-based) handles scheduling, QPS, finance at lower cost
- Technology stack must support scale: automated scheduling, digital inspections, real-time dashboards
- Complexity is not avoided -- it is organized through systems and processes
